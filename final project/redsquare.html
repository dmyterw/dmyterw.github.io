Whats Here?

Buildings

Bond Hall

A notoriously painful and confusing brick building. This building holds the following departments: College of Science and Engineering, Global Humanities and Religions, History, Linguistics, Mathematics, Philosophy. Fun fact, the Red Square "Viking View" webcam that overlooks Fisher Fountain is mounted on Bond Hall.

Haggard Hall

A brick building with a skybridge connection to Wilson Library. This building is the HQ of ATUS and contains Western's Print and Copy Services. A part of Western's libraries are also located within this building.

Humanities Building

A brick academic building that contains Human Resources.

Fraser Hall

A smaller brick building that contains a few lecture halls.

Miller Hall

A brick building that contains lecture halls, computer labs and the Miller Market. This building holds the following departments/academics: Center for International Studies, Human Services and Rehabilitation, Modern and Classical Languages, Teacher Education, Woodring College of Education, and Women Studies.

Sculptures

Fisher Fountain

A fountain created by WWU in honor of WWU President Charles Fisher in 1968.

Skyviewing Sculpture

A cube-shaped sculpture made from painted iron plate. Created by Isamu Noguchi in 1969

Lunar Drift

A wall mural of the lunar phases made from electronics, aluminum, glass, and archival inkjet prints on acrylic and mechatronics. Created by Rebecca Cummins and Paul DeMarinis in 2014.
